📋 Why Accurate Soil Data is Critical

  • Zero Values: NPK values of 0 indicate either untested soil or data entry error. Real soil always contains some nutrients.
  • Risk of Over-fertilization: Without accurate baseline data, recommendations may cause nutrient toxicity or environmental damage.
  • Cost Efficiency: Soil testing prevents wasteful spending on unnecessary fertilizers.
  • Crop-Specific Needs: Different crops require different nutrient levels. Accurate data ensures optimal growth.

💡 Next Steps

  • Soil Testing: Contact your local agricultural extension office or soil testing laboratory to get accurate NPK measurements.
  • Sample Collection: Collect soil samples from multiple locations in your field (6-8 inches deep) and mix thoroughly.
  • Professional Analysis: Lab results will provide precise NPK levels, pH, organic matter content, and micronutrient status.
  • Baseline Fertilization: If testing is delayed, apply balanced NPK fertilizer (like 10-10-10) at 250-300 kg/ha as emergency measure.
  • Organic Amendments: Add compost or farmyard manure (5-10 tons/ha) to improve overall soil health.
  • Return with Data: Once you have accurate soil test results, use this tool again for precise fertilizer recommendations.
